Needle Dropper — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 8, 2026

Needle Dropper (“the app”) helps you browse and manage your personal Discogs vinyl record collection and pick something to play. We designed it to keep your data on your device and to collect as little as possible. This policy explains what the app accesses and how it’s used.

Information the app accesses

  • Discogs account. When you choose to connect, the app uses Discogs’ official OAuth sign-in. We never see or store your Discogs password. The resulting access token is stored securely in the iOS Keychain on your device and is used only to fetch your collection from Discogs on your behalf.
  • Your collection data. Album details, cover art, ratings, and play history are cached locally on your device so the app works quickly and offline. This data is not sent to us or to any third party other than Discogs.
  • Camera. If you use the barcode scanner, the camera is used solely to read a record’s barcode so the app can look it up on Discogs. Images are processed on-device and are not stored or transmitted.
  • Motion. If you enable “shake to pick,” motion data is used on-device to detect a shake gesture. It is never stored or transmitted.

How information is used

Accessed information is used only to provide the app’s features: showing your collection, looking up records, and suggesting something to play. The app communicates with Discogs’ servers (api.discogs.com and i.discogs.com) to retrieve your collection and album artwork.

What we don’t do

  • We do not run ads or any third-party advertising or tracking SDKs.
  • We do not use analytics that identify you.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data.
  • We do not create accounts for you or collect data on our own servers.

Demo mode

You can explore the app without connecting an account by choosing “Browse with Demo Data.” Demo mode uses a built-in sample collection and accesses no personal information.

Data retention and deletion

All app data lives on your device. You can remove it at any time by disconnecting your Discogs account in Settings (which clears the cached collection and the stored token) or by deleting the app, which removes all associated data.

Third-party services

The app relies on Discogs to provide collection and catalog data. Your use of Discogs is governed by Discogs’ privacy policy.

Children’s privacy

Needle Dropper is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated date.
